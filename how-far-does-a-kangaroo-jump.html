27. A 6kg cat misjudges a jump and falls 5 meters. How much (gravitational) potential energy does the cat have? Show your solution.


The formula to calculate gravitational potential energy is:

PE = m.g.h

PE = potential energy

m = mass

g = gravitational acceleration

h = height.

Given that the cat has a mass of 6kg, falls 5 meters, and gravitational acceleration is 9.8 m/s^2, we can calculate the potential energy as follows:

PE = 6 kg x 9.8 m/s^2 x 5 m

PE = 294 Joules

Therefore, the cat has 294 Joules of gravitational potential energy.
