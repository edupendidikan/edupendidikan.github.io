4. Find the area of triangle​


First, find the area of the biggest triangle. With the given base and height, [tex] 18 + 8 = 26 [/tex] cm and [tex] 7 [/tex] cm, respectively, we get

[tex] \displaystyle \mathcal{A}_\text{biggest} = \frac{1}{2} \times 7 \times 26 = 91 [/tex]

and the smallest triangle, we have [tex] 8 [/tex] cm and [tex] 7 [/tex] cm, respectively, and yields

[tex] \displaystyle \mathcal{A}_{\text{smallest}} = \frac{1}{2} \times 8 \times 7 = 28 [/tex]

If we want to find the gray area, then we should subtract the biggest with the smallest one. And it gives us

[tex] \displaystyle \underset{\text{Gray}}{\mathcal{A}} = 91 - 28 = 63 [/tex]
